Soljanka

Link to the original recipe

Description

Soljanka is a hearty, flavorful Eastern European soup, popular in countries like Russia, Ukraine, and Germany. It's a tangy, slightly spicy dish made with a variety of meats (such as sausage, ham, or beef), pickles, tomatoes, and a rich, sour broth, typically flavored with smoked paprika, bay leaves, and sometimes olives. The soup is known for its bold combination of sour, salty, and savory flavors, and it's often served with a dollop of sour cream and fresh herbs.

Ingredients

  • 3 large onions, sliced into strips
  • 6 tbsp olive oil or sunflower oil
  • 3 red bell peppers, sliced into strips
  • 500 g sausage (e.g., hunter's sausage, bockwurst, ham, salami), cut into pieces
  • 1 Â½ liters broth
  • Tomato ketchup for thickening
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Spice seeds (e.g., mustard seeds, peppercorns, etc.)
  • 7 pickles, chopped
  • Tomato paste for thickening
  • Black peppercorns

Preparation

  • Preparation time: approx. 30 minutes
  • Total time: approx. 30 minutes

Heat the onion strips in a pot with a little oil. Add the bell pepper strips and finely chopped sausage pieces. Pour in the broth. Thicken with tomato ketchup and tomato paste, adjusting to taste. Next, add bay leaves, spice seeds, and peppercorns. Finally, add the finely chopped pickles. Let everything simmer for about 10-15 minutes over medium heat. Serve hot.
